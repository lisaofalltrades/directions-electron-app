DIY wooden stool ikea hack

As follow up to her popular DIY tree slice hairpin table tutorial, Caroline, founder of Burkatron, has graced us with another cool minialist furniture tutorial: the DIY wooden stool ikea hack!

To navigate from one step to the next you can use:

  • the navigation button ☰
  • the arrows ← → at the top of the page
  • the arrow keys ◀ ▶ on your keyboard to move from one step to the next

All photos and directions belong to Burkatron.

Supplies

  1. Marius Stool from Ikea
  2. slab of wood (approx 35 x 35 cm)
  3. hand sander
  4. longer screws (at least 5cm in length depending on the thickness of the wood slice)
  5. power drill

Source a Wood Slice

Source a wood slice. You can try your local lumber yard or garden centers. As well as online. Pictured is a wood slice approx 35 x 35cm.

Smooth It Down

Lightly sand down the top with a hand sander. You could also remove the bark and sand the sides as well, but I prefer the natural finish.

Attach Legs

Screw the stool legs directly onto the bottom of your wood slice. The screws that come with the stool are too short so switch to longer ones- at least 5cm length depending on the thickness of the wood slice! (you don't need the ikea stool seat- you can recycle it if you don't want it.)

Voilà

Flip the stool over and it's ready to use. You can treat or stain the wood but i'm going to leave mine unfinished for now.
